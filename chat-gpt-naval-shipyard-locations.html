Are San Diego, Charleston, and Groton Naval Shipyards?

Short answer: Only Groton is an active naval shipyard today. San Diego is not a shipyard, and Charleston was one but closed decades ago.

Here’s the breakdown:

San Diego

  • Not a naval shipyard.

  • San Diego has Naval Base San Diego, which is a major fleet homeport, not a construction or heavy-repair shipyard.

  • Some private yards (e.g., NASSCO) do shipbuilding/repair in San Diego, but they are not naval shipyards.

Charleston (South Carolina)

  • Was a naval shipyard, but no longer.

  • The Charleston Naval Shipyard operated from 1901 to 1996.

  • It closed as part of the post-Cold War base realignment (BRAC).

  • The site is now a mixed commercial/government facility (e.g., Coast Guard, private ship repair companies).

Groton (Connecticut)

  • Yes—this is a current shipyard.

  • Groton is home to General Dynamics Electric Boat, which builds and overhauls U.S. Navy submarines.

  • It is one of the two primary U.S. submarine shipyards (the other is Newport News in Virginia).
